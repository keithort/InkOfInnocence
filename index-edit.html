Our Mission
There are 2.4 million people in jail in the United States. An estimated 100,000 are innocent. Thousands are serving long sentences for crimes they didn’t commit- because one critical piece of evidence is being ignored. Their DNA. Social status is one of the main barriers for getting access to this expensive but conclusive method.
To shine a spotlight on the evidence that could set the innocent free we are collecting DNA of the wrongly convicted and turning it into ink. Ink used to give the innocent a face…containing DNA evidence… Posters, newspaper ads, a letter to the Governor, a pen, a signature on a petition all containing DNA evidence. We are not just giving the wrongly convicted a face, we will be putting their DNA in the hands of those who have the power to balance the scales and give equal access to DNA testing. Our campaign doesn’t just state their innocence. It can be used to prove it.
How It Works
Every drop of our ink contains the DNA of the wrongly convicted.
Step 1
DNA Collection
We research and collect the DNA from wrongfully convicted prisoners.
Step 2
Turn into ink
A lab takes the prisoners DNA and uses it to create our ink.
Step 3
Send letters
We use the ink to spread our message to lawmakers, encouraging change.
