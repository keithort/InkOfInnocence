Our Mission

There are 2.4 million people in jail in the United States. An estimated 100,000 are innocent. Thousands are serving long sentences for crimes they didn’t commit- because one critical piece of evidence is being ignored. Their DNA. Social status is one of the main barriers for getting access to this expensive but conclusive method. And to shine a spotlight on the evidence that could set the innocent free, we are collecting DNA of the wrongfully convicted and turning it into ink.

Ink used to give the innocent a face containing DNA evidence, posters, newspaper ads, a letter to the Governor, a pen, a signature on a petition, all containing DNA evidence. So we are not just giving the wrongfully convicted a face, we will be putting their DNA in the hands of those who have the power to balance the scales of justice and give equal access to DNA testing. Our campaign doesn’t just state their innocence. It can be used to help prove it.

How It Works
Every drop of our ink contains the DNA of the wrongfully convicted.
Step 1
DNA Collection
We research and collect DNA from wrongfully convicted prisoners.
Step 2
Turn into ink
A lab takes the prisoner DNA and uses it to create our ink.
Step 3
Promote Justice
The ink is used to spread our message and bring justice to the wrongfully convicted.
