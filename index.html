Our Mission
Up to 100,000 people in United States' prisons are innocent. Thousands are serving sentences for crimes they did not commit, because they cannot afford access to the one piece of evidence that can set them free… DNA testing. To shine a spotlight on the evidence that could set the innocent free, we are collecting prisoner DNA and partnering with DNA Technologies in Halifax, Canada. We are turning the DNA into ink. Ink used to give the innocent a face, containing DNA evidence. Posters and newspaper ads containing DNA evidence. A letter to the governor, a signature on a petition, all written with ink containing DNA evidence. So we are not only getting their voices heard. We will be putting their DNA in the hands of those who have the power to balance the scales and give equal access to DNA testing. Our campaign doesn’t just state their innocence. It could be used to help prove it.
How It Works
Every drop of our ink contains the DNA of the wrongfully convicted.
Step 1
DNA Collection
We research and collect the DNA from wrongfully convicted prisoners.
Step 2
Turn into ink
A lab takes the prisoner DNA and uses it to create our ink.
Step 3
Promote Justice
The ink is used to spread our message and bring justice to the wrongfully convicted.
